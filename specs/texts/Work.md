# Work (`/work`)

## Introduction

I am a design engineer by discipline. In previous chapters: editorial design, 3D animation, and a period spent making Flash animations and multimedia CD-ROMs that no longer exist anywhere. I have always believed in working across domains. A specialist in one thing tends to miss the connections.

These are selected projects from the last two decades.

## Professional

### XING Design System

_Web Design / Web Development / Writing_

As Senior Frontend Engineer on the Design System team, I built and maintained more than 50 components in [React](https://reactjs.org/), [TypeScript](https://www.typescriptlang.org/), and [Styled Components](https://styled-components.com/)—collaborating with designers on specifications, writing documentation, and supporting product teams across the [XING platform](https://www.xing.com/). The system supported dark and light modes and scaled across device types.

### XING Design System—Browser Extension

_Web Development_

A browser extension that allows product managers, designers, and engineers to identify Design System components directly on the platform—making visible what is custom-built and what is shared. Built in 2024.

### XING Onboarding Illustrations

_Illustration / Icon Design_

Three illustrations for an onboarding screen—Job Beginner, Professional, and Retired—were conceived, drafted, and completed within the XING illustration brand guidelines. Concept to final in a single day.

## Personal—Web & Design

### The Traditional Colors of Japan

_Design / Web Development_

In 2007, I found a Japanese book listing 250 traditional color values with their exact codes. I turned the appendix into an ASE file compatible with standard design software.

[View project](https://www.stefanimhoff.de/traditional-colors-of-japan/) →

### iA Writer Templates

_Design / Web Development_

The writing template **Nanzan (南山)** for [iA Writer](https://ia.net/writer) is inspired by the typographic style of _Engaging Japanese Philosophy_ by Thomas P. Kasulis. It was designed by the Nanzan Institute for Religion and Culture.

The writing template **Shibui (渋い)** draws from the Japanese aesthetic of shibui, characterized by the quiet, unforced beauty of the Muromachi period. This visual language also influences the design of this website, a martial arts book, and a photoblog.

- [Download Nanzan](https://github.com/kogakure/ia-writer-template-nanzan) →
- [Download Shibui](https://github.com/kogakure/ia-writer-template-shibui) →

### Bamboo Illustration

_Illustration_

Created in 2006 for my martial arts website—originally in Adobe Illustrator using the gradient mesh tool. Rebuilt in 2015 as SVG using linear and radial gradients in Affinity, as mesh gradients are unsupported in the format.

### Koi Illustration

_Illustration_

Made for an earlier version of this site in 2007. The source is the Japanese tattoo tradition — _Irezumi_ (刺青) — and a legend in which koi swimming upstream against magical falls are transformed, over centuries, into dragons. Drawn in pencil, finished in Photoshop with a Wacom tablet.

### Ninjutsu Book—木隠

_Web Design / Web Development / Writing_

My oldest work, first published in 1999. The current version is the ninth iteration. In 2022 I translated the full text into English. The site holds several decades of research into Ninja and the art of Ninjutsu.

[Visit project](https://www.kogakure.de/en) →

## Client Work

### Osteopathie-Institut Hamburg

_Web Design / Web Development / Photography_

One of the last client sites I built before focusing entirely on product work at XING. The school's teacher was my martial arts instructor at the time. The materials—photography, text, and quotes—were exceptional, and the work reflects that.

### Thai Restaurants Hamburg

_Graphic Design / Web Design / Web Development_

A site for three Thai restaurants in Hamburg, built in 2010. It ran for nearly ten years. I studied Thai visual culture closely—its colors, its ornamental logic—and tried to honor that in the design.
