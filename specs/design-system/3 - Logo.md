## Philosophy

The mark draws from the tradition of the Japanese _hanko_—the personal seal used for centuries to authenticate, sign, and claim authorship. Enclosed within a perfect circle, the letterforms are abstracted into a dense, interlocking composition that reads simultaneously as initials and as a single unified glyph. The circle is not decoration; it is containment, intention, and completeness. Like a seal pressed into paper, it carries weight.

## Voice & Tone

The logo speaks without words. It does not explain itself. It is confident in its opacity—legible to those who look closely, abstract to those who do not. This ambiguity is intentional. A mark that rewards attention is a mark worth returning to. The deep crimson red (_beni_, 紅) is the only color it ever appears in—a deliberate reference to the ink of the stamp, and to the quiet authority that comes with it.

## Variants

| Variant          | Use case                                               | Description                                                                                                       |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Primary mark** | Hero placement, about pages, print                     | Full-size circular seal — the default.                                                                            |
| **Small mark**   | Navigation, favicon, social avatars                    | Reduced version of the same seal at small sizes. Ensure it remains legible at its minimum size.                   |
| **Mark on type** | Formal contexts where the name must accompany the mark | Seal paired with “Stefan Imhoff” set in Switzer Variable, Regular, with letterspacing matching the caption style. |

## Clear Space

The minimum clear space around the mark equals the radius of the `inner circle ÷ 4` on all sides. No text, imagery, or other graphic elements may encroach on this zone. This breathing room is _ma_—it is part of the mark, not around it.

## Minimum Sizes

| Context         | Minimum diameter                             |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Print (300 dpi) | 12 mm                                        |
| Screen          | 24 px                                        |
| Favicon         | 16 × 16 px (simplified rendering acceptable) |

Below these thresholds, detail is lost and the mark no longer functions. Do not scale below minimum.

## Color on Light/Dark Backgrounds

| Background                   | Mark colour            | Notes                                                                                                 |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Light (`#E6E6E6` or lighter) | Crimson Red `#900B20`  | Primary usage. 7,46:1 contrast ratio — WCAG AAA.                                                      |
| Dark (`#0E0D0C` or similar)  | `#E6E6E6` (light grey) | Inverted for dark contexts. Never use crimson on near-black — the 2,08:1 ratio fails all WCAG levels. |
| Crimson Red background       | `#E6E6E6` (light grey) | Reserved for special contexts (e.g. a printed seal).                                                  |

## Placement

The mark is placed in the top-left corner of the site layout, anchored to the grid. In print, it sits in the upper-left quadrant or centered, depending on the format. It never floats unanchored.

## Unacceptable Uses

- Do not stretch, rotate, or skew the mark.
- Do not add drop shadows, gradients, bevels, or outlines.
- Do not change the stroke weight of the internal linework.
- Do not place the crimson mark on a busy photographic background.
- Do not rearrange or isolate elements of the internal composition.
- Do not redraw or approximate the mark—always use the original vector file.
- Do not lock it up with third-party logos on the same visual plane.
