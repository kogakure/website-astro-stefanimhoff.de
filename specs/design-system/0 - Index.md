# Ma (間) Design System

## Introduction

The space between. Two letters. Instantly memorable. The entire design system is built on the idea that the empty space _is_ the design. It also means “gate with light passing through”—which is literally what a well-designed interface does.

- **Two letters**—works everywhere: package names, CLI tools, documentation, conversation (“check the Ma docs”).
- **The core idea**—grid, spacing tokens, whitespace-heavy layouts, typographic breathing room, motion philosophy—they are all expressions of _ma_.
- **Visually striking**—the kanji 間 (gate + sun) could appear alongside the Hanko mark in documentation
- **Philosophically precise**—it names the _absence_ that makes the presence meaningful, which is precisely what a design system does: it defines the space so the content can exist within it.
- **Not overused**—unlike “zen” or “wabi-sabi,” _ma_ hasn't been co-opted by every wellness brand

The system would read naturally: _“Ma Design System,”_ or simply _“Ma”_—the space between.

## Closing Note—_Shibui_ 渋い

> In its core aesthetic sense, shibui denotes a simple yet profound elegance marked by austerity and conscious restraint, evoking unpretentious beauty through balanced simplicity and subtle depth. This quality prioritizes economy in expression, fostering a timeless tranquility that avoids ostentation while revealing intrinsic refinement over time.—[Grokipedia, “Shibui”](https://grokipedia.com/page/Shibui)

This guideline is itself an expression of the principles it describes. It is not exhaustive by ambition—it is restrained by conviction. The seven principles—asymmetry (_fukinsei_ 不均齊), simplicity (_kanso_ 簡素), austerity (_koko_ 考古), naturalness (_shizen_ 自然), subtlety (_yūgen_ 幽玄), detachment (_datsuzoku_ 脱俗), and tranquility (_seijaku_ 静寂)—are not decorative themes applied to a design system. They are the design system’s architecture, its reason for existing, and its measure of success.
