## Philosophy

The mark draws from the tradition of the Japanese _hanko_ — the personal seal used for centuries to authenticate, sign, and claim authorship. Enclosed within a perfect circle, the letterforms are abstracted into a dense, interlocking composition that reads simultaneously as initials and as a single unified glyph. The circle is not decoration; it is containment, intention, and completeness. Like a seal pressed into paper, it carries weight.

The deep crimson red (_beni_, 紅) is the only hue the mark wears — base Beni on light grounds, Beni Light on dark. A deliberate reference to the ink of the stamp, and to the quiet authority that comes with it.

## Size Scale

Below 24px screen / 12mm print, detail is lost and the mark no longer functions.

## Clear Space

Minimum clear space = inner circle radius ÷ 4 on all sides. No text, imagery, or graphic elements may enter this zone. This breathing room is _ma_ — it is part of the mark.

## Unacceptable Uses

<LogoDontList />

- Do not stretch, rotate, or skew the mark.
- Do not add shadows, gradients, bevels, or outlines.
- Do not change the internal stroke weight.
- Do not place the crimson mark on a busy photographic background.
- Do not rearrange or isolate internal elements.
- Do not redraw or approximate — always use the original vector file.
