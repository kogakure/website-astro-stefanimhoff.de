## Purpose—_Kanso_ 簡素

To create work that removes confusion and decoration—interfaces, systems, and writing that serve their purpose with nothing left to take away. Nearly two decades at the intersection of design and code have led to a single conviction: the discipline of leaving things out is the discipline that matters most.

## Vision—_Yūgen_ 幽玄

To build a body of work—digital, written, and designed—that suggests rather than declares. Work that rewards sustained attention; that reveals new layers over time, like a shibui object whose beauty compounds with each encounter.

## Positioning

A design engineer who operates at the seam between visual design and front-end engineering—building interfaces that hold up under scrutiny, informed by Swiss precision and Japanese restraint.

## Audience Segments

- **Primary:** Design and engineering teams seeking a collaborator who understands both sides—someone who can design the system and build it.
- **Secondary:** Readers interested in the intersection of design, code, philosophy, and the examined life.
- **Tertiary:** The broader design community encountering the work through portfolios, conference talks, or journal essays.

## Personality/Attributes

| Attribute         | What it means                | What it is not     |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------ |
| Precise           | Every decision is deliberate | Rigid or cold      |
| Restrained        | Says more with less          | Timid or invisible |
| Thoughtful        | Considers the long view      | Slow or indecisive |
| Grounded          | Rooted in craft, not trend   | Old-fashioned      |
| Quietly confident | The work speaks first        | Boastful           |
