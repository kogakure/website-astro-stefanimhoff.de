I am **Stefan Imhoff**—design engineer, writer, and
observer—based in Hamburg, Germany.

I find meaning in ideas. [Books](https://www.goodreads.com/kogakure) on philosophy, science, and economics. [Podcasts](https://raindrop.io/kogakure/podcasts-24118384). [Films](https://www.themoviedb.org/u/kogakure/favorites)—particularly [Korean](https://www.themoviedb.org/list/8216385-korea?view=grid) and [Japanese](https://www.themoviedb.org/list/106001-japan?view=grid) cinema and drama. I believe humans understand the world through narrative,
and I try to contribute to that—through [essays](/writing/), sketchnotes, [Haiku](/haiku/), and a free [book on Ninja and Ninjutsu](https://www.kogakure.de/en/).

Outside the screen: [walking and cycling through Hamburg](https://hamburg.stefanimhoff.de/), calisthenics, ink sketching and watercolour, and a persistent attention to the small things—the quality of light through leaves (<Ruby base="木" text="こ" /><Ruby base="漏" text="も" /><Ruby base="れ" /><Ruby	base="日"	text="び" />), the sound of a storm, the particular silence after it passes.

I practice minimalism, draw from Stoic philosophy, and hold Japanese
aesthetics close—especially the principle of _shibui_ (<Ruby base="渋い" />): quiet beauty that reveals itself slowly.
